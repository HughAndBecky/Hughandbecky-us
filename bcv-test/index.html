Biography

I use tools from language documentation and linguistics to create corpora of oral stories. These corpora are then used to demonstrate the natural grammatical patterns used within the community. Using a collaborative process, I marshal these oral stories from recording through to transcription and translation.

I write about how unique elements of the grammar are used by speakers to help the rest of the world understand the capacity of human language and diversity in the human race.

My research interests include African languages, morphosyntax, language description and documentation, diachronic morphosyntax, and discourse grammar. I am particularly interested in the morphosyntax of the Kainji languages of Nigeria and how the can inform our understanding of the Niger-Congo language family more broadly.
